About

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Department of Bioengineering and Penn Institute for Computational Science at the University of Pennsylvania.

My research interests lie in the intersection of oncology, machine learning, and mechanistic modeling. My research is on creating computational representations of cancer development, tumor microenvironment interactions, and treatment effects for use in downstream science and clinical tasks, particularly aiding hypothesis generation and identifying therapeutic strategies.

I have done internships at the Harvard Department of Biomedical Informatics, MIT McGovern Institute, Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory and Mathworks. I received my PhD in Bioengineering at the University of Pennsylvania, advised by Dr. Ravi Radhakrishnan. I received a B.S. in Biomedical Engineering and Computer Engineering from Johns Hopkins University in 2014.

I have previously served as Co-President of the Penn Postdoc Association and Co-President of the Penn Science Policy and Diplomacy Group. I am currently a member of the board of the National Postdoc Association and an associate fellow at the Leonard Davis Institute for Health Economics.
